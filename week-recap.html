Laredo United South delivered one of the most dominant district performances of the young season, sweeping rival Laredo Alexander by a combined score of 16-1. Alexander has long been the power in District 30-6A, making this sweep a massive statement for United South's 2026 ambitions. The Panthers' pitching staff was lights-out across both games, and the offense manufactured runs early and often. This is the kind of series win that earns respect statewide — and it could shake up the South Texas 6A rankings heading into the heart of district play.

Elite Performances

Player Spotlights

⚡ Pitcher of the Week

Anthony DiCello — Clear Creek

RHP · District 24-6A · Houston Region
6 IP 1 R 5 H Win

DiCello was masterful against Deer Park, working six innings while limiting the damage to a single run. His command and ability to go deep into the game gave Clear Creek the district win they needed. With closer Sauers finishing it off, this was a team pitching effort that shows what Clear Creek's staff is capable of in 2026.

🌟 Draft Watch

Grady Emerson — Fort Worth Christian

SS/LHP · DFW Region · Projected Top-5 MLB Draft Pick
Top-5 Draft Projection 2-Way Star TXHSB #1 Overall

Emerson continues to be the most-watched player in Texas high school baseball. The left-handed hitting shortstop from Fort Worth Christian is a legitimate candidate for the #1 overall pick in the 2026 MLB Draft, drawing comparisons to premium two-way talent. Every game he plays this spring is a showcase event for pro scouts. He's the crown jewel of the DFW region and arguably the best prep player in the country.

🔥 Prospect Alert

Cooper Harris — RHP

RHP · Texas Commit · Day-1 Draft Consideration
Texas Commit 2026 Draft Eligible Power Arm

Harris remains one of the state's most intriguing arms. The Texas recruit is drawing Day-1 draft consideration and continues to build his case with every start. A power pitcher with projection, he's one of those guys MLB teams are going to have a serious conversation about in June.

💎 Sophomore Phenom

Lubin Rincon — Shadow Creek (Pearland)

SS/3B · #1 Texas 2027 Prospect · #3 National
Texas Commit Class of 2027 #3 National

Rincon is only a sophomore and already the most talked-about young player in the state. The Venezuela-born shortstop at Shadow Creek/Pearland is the #1 Texas prospect in the 2027 class and #3 in the country. Every week he takes the field, pro scouts are watching. He projects as a future top-5 MLB Draft pick and is the kind of talent that comes around once in a generation for Texas high school baseball.

What's Coming

Teams to Watch Next Week

Tomball — Can the streak continue?

At 19-0-1, Tomball is the hottest team in Texas. With ace Karson Reeder (Texas commit) and two-way stud CJ Sampson (Texas A&M commit) leading the way, the Cougars face the challenge of staying unbeaten through the back half of district. They've been the most dominant team in Houston and arguably the state. A loss would be shocking at this point.

19-0-1 | VYPE #1 Houston

Laredo United South — Real deal?

After sweeping Alexander 16-1 combined, United South has to be considered the favorite in District 30-6A. The question now is whether they can sustain this level of performance through the rest of district and into the playoffs. If their pitching holds, this team could make some serious noise in South Texas 6A.

District 30-6A Leaders

Kingwood — Defending champs heating up

The defending 6A-DII state champions are building toward another playoff run with Graham Charboneau (Baylor signee, 2025 State MVP) leading as a senior. Kingwood is the measuring stick in the Houston region — every team wants to be the one to knock them off. Their second half of district will be must-watch baseball.

Defending State Champs

Iowa Park — 4A dark horse

Don't sleep on Iowa Park. The TABC #4-ranked Hawks are rolling through district and look like a legitimate threat to reach the 4A state tournament. Their 12-0 demolition of Vernon this week showed they're not just surviving — they're dominating. Keep this program on your radar as playoff seeding takes shape.
